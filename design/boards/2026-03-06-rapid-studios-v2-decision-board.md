## Decision Sections

- Decision area: Scope model
- Option references:
  - homepage-first refinement cycle
  - full-site board before new Figma frames exist
- Recommended option:
  - homepage-first refinement cycle
- Reasoning:
  - The current Figma file only contains a usable homepage blueprint. Driving the next decisions from that reality keeps the pipeline honest and prevents fake precision for pages that still lack visual source material.
- Final choice:
  - Pending approval

- Decision area: Hero tone and structure
- Option references:
  - original Mobbin hero cluster
  - Figma homepage blueprint `1:2`
- Recommended option:
  - oversized editorial headline, short support copy, 2-button CTA row, and proof-oriented right rail
- Reasoning:
  - This is the strongest overlap between the approved research direction and the actual Figma blueprint. It creates a premium first impression without turning the hero into a feature list.
- Final choice:
  - Pending approval

- Decision area: Header model
- Option references:
  - short sticky nav with one persistent CTA
  - pill shell with dual CTA cluster from the Figma blueprint
- Recommended option:
  - pill shell header with `Work`, `Services`, `Process`, `Contact`, plus `View work` and `Start a project`
- Reasoning:
  - The current blueprint already establishes this as the clearest navigation identity. It also matches the approved recommendation for short, CTA-aware navigation.
- Final choice:
  - Pending approval

- Decision area: Homepage card density
- Option references:
  - metric-heavy or tag-heavy cards
  - simple cards with pale media wells and short copy
- Recommended option:
  - simple cards with pale media wells and concise copy
- Reasoning:
  - The blueprint looks strongest when cards stay calm and structural. The more the homepage cards act like mini dashboards, the further the implementation drifts from the approved visual rhythm.
- Final choice:
  - Pending approval

- Decision area: Featured work treatment
- Option references:
  - detailed portfolio cards with multiple metadata blocks
  - editorial cards with one image well, title, and one-line outcome
- Recommended option:
  - editorial cards with one image well, title, and one-line outcome
- Reasoning:
  - This keeps the homepage selective and premium. Detailed metrics can still live in the case-study pages where the visitor is already committed to reading deeper.
- Final choice:
  - Pending approval

- Decision area: Process density
- Option references:
  - 5-step homepage process
  - 3-step homepage process plus full process page
- Recommended option:
  - 3-step homepage process plus full process page
- Reasoning:
  - The homepage blueprint clearly favors a compact 3-card row. The full 5-step explanation still belongs on `/process`, where more depth is useful.
- Final choice:
  - Pending approval

- Decision area: Homepage CTA band
- Option references:
  - project-start CTA plus email fallback
  - email-first CTA plus secondary work link
- Recommended option:
  - email-first CTA plus secondary work link on the homepage
- Reasoning:
  - That pattern matches the current blueprint and keeps the final homepage conversion moment concise. The stronger project-start CTA can still remain in the hero and contact page.
- Final choice:
  - Pending approval

- Decision area: State coverage priority
- Option references:
  - continue high-fidelity work first and add states later
  - promote states into the next structure and design gates
- Recommended option:
  - promote states into the next gates immediately
- Reasoning:
  - The phase 6 analysis shows state coverage is the clearest design weakness right now. Treating states as a design input now will reduce rework later in Figma and code.
- Final choice:
  - Pending approval

- Decision area: Font system
- Option references:
  - inherit placeholder Figma typography
  - lock to `Space Grotesk` plus `Manrope`
- Recommended option:
  - lock to `Space Grotesk` plus `Manrope`
- Reasoning:
  - The approved token system already defines the correct typographic direction. Allowing the placeholder font stack to persist would make later fidelity work noisier and less consistent.
- Final choice:
  - Pending approval

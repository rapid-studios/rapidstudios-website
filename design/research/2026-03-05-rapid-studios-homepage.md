# Research Report

- Topic: Rapid Studios marketing website homepage
- Date: 2026-03-05
- Platform: Web
- Audience: Prospective clients, collaborators, and future hires
- Goal: Establish a strong direction for a modern studio website before writing the design spec

## Assumption

This first pass assumes `rapidstudios-website` is a marketing site for a creative or product-focused studio. If the target is instead a SaaS product, portfolio microsite, or app shell, approve the pattern work only in part and redirect the next research pass.

## Sources

- [Instrument](https://www.instrument.com/)
  - Why it is relevant: Strong brand, product, and marketing integration with clear work-first storytelling.
- [BASIC/DEPT](https://www.basicagency.com/)
  - Why it is relevant: High-contrast editorial presentation, cultural positioning, and prominent featured engagements.
- [Clay](https://clay.global/)
  - Why it is relevant: Clear service taxonomy, strong trust signals, and balanced brand-plus-delivery messaging.
- [Metalab](https://www.metalab.com/)
  - Why it is relevant: Extremely concise positioning and a stripped-down homepage hierarchy.
- [Fantasy Services](https://fantasy.co/services/)
  - Why it is relevant: Strong capability framing and polished category structure for high-end digital consulting.

## Pattern Clusters

### Layout

- Evidence: The strongest studio sites open with one dominant hero, then move quickly into featured work, capability framing, and proof. Instrument emphasizes recent work early, BASIC/DEPT foregrounds featured engagements, Clay combines services with client proof, and Metalab strips the structure down to the essentials.
- Recommendation: Use a homepage with this order:
  1. Hero statement and primary CTA
  2. Featured work or proof of capability
  3. Service pillars
  4. Trust strip
  5. Approach or process
  6. Final contact CTA

### Visual Hierarchy

- Evidence: Instrument and Metalab rely on a simple, declarative headline. BASIC/DEPT adds more editorial energy, while Clay and Fantasy support the hero with clearer service framing. Across all of them, large typography and disciplined spacing do more work than dense copy.
- Recommendation: Lead with one sharp statement, one supporting paragraph, and a single primary action. Keep section copy tight and let case studies, logos, and visual modules carry the depth.

### Navigation

- Evidence: The common pattern is a short top-level nav with explicit work and service paths. Contact is always easy to reach. The best sites avoid sprawling menus.
- Recommendation: Keep the primary nav to `Work`, `Services`, `About`, `Insights`, and `Contact`. Add a persistent CTA such as `Start a project` only if the brand voice supports it.

### States

- Evidence: These sites rely on subtle transitions, hover states, and media reveals rather than heavy animation systems. Motion supports polish and navigation but does not dominate the content.
- Recommendation: Use restrained motion:
  - fade and slight translate on section reveal
  - clear hover feedback on case study cards
  - optional reel or media motion only if it supports the brand story
  - prioritize performance and mobile readability over spectacle

### Content Model

- Evidence: The best examples avoid generic service-card overload. They show a point of view, back it with work, and then explain capabilities. Clay is especially clear on service breadth; BASIC/DEPT and Instrument are stronger on brand positioning and credibility.
- Recommendation: Build the homepage around:
  - positioning statement
  - 2-4 featured projects
  - three to five capability pillars
  - selected clients or outcomes
  - short methodology or approach
  - final conversion block

## Recommended Direction

- Summary: Combine Metalab's clarity, Instrument's integrated brand-product framing, Clay's service clarity, and BASIC/DEPT's editorial confidence.
- Why it fits: That blend is appropriate for a studio site because it gives Rapid Studios a distinct voice without sacrificing clarity or credibility.

## Proposed Homepage Structure

1. Hero
   - One-line positioning statement
   - Supporting sentence
   - Primary CTA plus optional secondary `View work`
2. Featured work
   - Two large case studies plus one or two secondary cards
3. Services
   - Clear pillars such as brand, website, product, or growth experience
4. Trust
   - Client logos, outcomes, or selected partnerships
5. Approach
   - How Rapid Studios works and what makes the team different
6. Insights or point of view
   - Optional, if the brand needs authority-building content
7. Contact CTA
   - Clear closing invitation

## Risks And Open Questions

- Risk: The site could drift into generic agency cliches if the hero copy is vague.
- Risk: Too many service buckets will dilute the message and weaken the homepage.
- Risk: Overuse of motion could make the site feel expensive but unfocused.
- Question: Is Rapid Studios positioning itself as a creative studio, product studio, development partner, or a hybrid?
- Question: Do we already have case studies, logos, or testimonials that can anchor the homepage?
- Question: Should the visual tone feel bold and experimental or more premium and restrained?
